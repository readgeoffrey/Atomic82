Why you need to test for lead

Lead pipes are everywhere

You heard what happened in Flint, Michigan. But many people don’t realize this is a nation-wide issue. Reuters found over 3,000 communities across the United States with lead poisoning rates double - or worse - than Flint.

Lead poisoning ruins lives

Health experts all agree: Lead is terrible for your brain and body. It hinders brain function, stunts growth in children, and has other long-term health implications. Though the EPA set an acceptable threshhold of lead in water, there is no “good” amount of lead. It's especially dangerous for infants, children, the elderly, and the infirm.

Government testing doesn't catch everything

Most water testing done in communities is done at the local treatment plant. However, water then travels from there through city water mains, to your house's water pipes, and out your sink. Lead from any of these pipes could contaminate your water before you drink it.

Children younger than 6 years are especially vulnerable to lead poisoning, which can severely affect mental and physical development. At very high levels, lead poisoning can be fatal.

Mayo Clinic

Here's how it works

We mail you a tube

After you order, we'll send you a sampling kit with a bottle to fill up from your sink. We'll also include some simple instructions explaining how to take a good sample.

Fill it with water and send it back

Put your water-filled sample bottle back in the box, and apply the pre-printed mailing label (shipping is on us!). Leave it on your porch for your postal worker to pick up.

We email you the results

You'll get the test results emailed back to you in under a week. Instead of the standard complicated, overwhelming scientific report labs usually send, we'll provide you with a clear and intuitive explanation of how much lead we found in your water, and more importantly - tips on what to do next!

About Us

We are two guys who have spent years working in and around government to make public services better. After the stories from Flint, Michigan hit the headlines, we started worrying about our water. We wanted to make sure our water was safe to drink. We wanted to make sure our family's water was safe to drink.

We found a number of testing kits online and in stores, but those weren't very trustworthy. Then we tried to contact our local water testing labs, but ordering a single test was complicated.
