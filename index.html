Your water, it turns out, is rather important.

Test your water today

Lead-in-water is, unfortunately, a problem in more than just Flint.

Old houses

In 1986 Congress passed a law banning the use of lead water pipes in new homes. The problem is that most of us live in houses built before 1986.

Insufficient Testing

Most water testing done in communities is done at the treatment plant. Water then travels from there through city water mains, through your house's water pipes, and through your water fixtures. Each place is a point at which stuff can get into your water before you drink it.

Uncertain Regulations

The EPA regulates lead levels in water. They allow for certain amounts of lead in your water before it is considered "toxic". Meanwhile, health experts agree: Any level of lead consumption is unsafe. Especially for newborns, children, the elderly, or the infirm.

How do we test?

We don't use some crappy testing strip - we send your water sample to our professional, government certified testing labs. They run their tests to look for lead using advanced, state of the art testing equipment. They can find trace amounts of lead down to 1 part per billion!

We know you probably aren't a scientist. So after the lab geeks do their analysis, we send you an easy to understand report, complete with recommendations for what to do next.
