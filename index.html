Why you need to test for lead

Old pipes are everywhere

Most of us have heard of Flint. But a lot of us don’t realize that this is a nation-wide issue. In fact, a recent Reuter’s study found 1,100 communities across the country have lead-in-water issues as bad or worse than the Michigan town.

Lead is not good.

Health experts all agree: Lead is terrible for your brain and body. It hinders brain function, stunts growth in children, and has other long-term health implications. Though the EPA says that some (up to 15 parts per billion) lead is ok, there is no “good” level of lead. Especially for infants, children, and those with particular health conditions who are more susceptible to contaminants.

Reason 3

The EPA regulates lead levels in water. They allow for certain amounts of lead in your water before it is considered "toxic". Meanwhile, health experts agree: Any level of lead consumption is unsafe. Especially for newborns, children, the elderly, or the infirm.

Doesn't the government monitor this stuff?

Old houses: In 1986 Congress passed a law banning the use of lead water pipes in new homes. The problem is that most of us live in houses built before 1986.

Insufficient testing: Most water testing done in communities is done at the treatment plant. Water then travels from there through city water mains, through your house's water pipes, and through your water fixtures. Each place is a point at which stuff can get into your water before you drink it.

Lax Regulations: The EPA regulates lead levels in water. They allow for certain amounts of lead in your water before it is considered "toxic". Meanwhile, health experts agree: Any level of lead consumption is unsafe. Especially for newborns, children, the elderly, or the infirm.

About Us

We are two guys who have spent years working in and around government to make public services better. After the stories from Flint, Michigan hit the headlines, we started worrying about our water. We wanted to make sure our water was safe to drink. We wanted to make sure our family's water was safe to drink.

We found a number of testing kits online and in stores, but those weren't very good. Then we tried to contact our local water testing labs, but ordering a single test was complicated. Government labs were too slow, if they exist at all. Commercial labs were too expensive. Both mainly work with industry and companies.

So we made a company.

We've figured out how to work with professional, government certified labs so you don't have to. We can take advantage of volume pricing to get you cheaper tests.

We can simplify a messy world, so you can better understand how your family's health is effected by what's coming out of your tap.
